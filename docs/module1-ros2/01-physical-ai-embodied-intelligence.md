## Introduction ğŸŒŸ

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift from traditional digital artificial intelligence to systems that interact with and operate within the physical world. Unlike conventional AI that processes abstract data in virtual environments, Physical AI systems must navigate the complexities of real-world physics, sensory uncertainties, and dynamic environments. This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of Physical AI and embodied intelligence, establishing the foundation for understanding how robots perceive, reason, and act in physical spaces.

Embodied intelligence is the theory that intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment. Rather than processing information in isolation, embodied systems leverage their physical form and environmental interactions to exhibit intelligent behavior. This perspective fundamentally changes how we approach robotics and AI, emphasizing the importance of the body-mind-environment coupling in generating intelligent behavior.

The emergence of Physical AI is driven by the need for autonomous systems that can operate effectively in unstructured, real-world environments. From household robots that navigate cluttered homes to industrial systems that adapt to changing manufacturing conditions, Physical AI systems must handle uncertainty, variability, and the inherent messiness of the real world.
